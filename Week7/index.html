1. Flatness

Imagine being stuck in a movie theatre forever, only seeing flickering shadows on the screen. That is the life of a prisoner in Plato's cave story. Chained up, facing a wall, all they see are weird, moving shapes. They have no idea what is causing them, but they believe these shadows are the real deal, the divine. The prisoners are giving these shadows names, putting them on a pedestal. They may call the shadow of a dog a "barking monster" because that is what they know with their limited knowledge. This limited and narrowed is referred to by a scholar Nick Sousanis as "Flatness". It is like seeing the world in a flat picture. There is no depth or complexity. These cave dwellers are stuck in a Flatland because they only have access to this two-dimensional view of reality. Their minds cannot comprehend the light that creates these shadows, albeit the world outside the cave with all its sunshine, trees, and actual dogs. Envision it like this: using only their eyes, like watching a silent movie, they do not get the full picture because they cannot hear the sounds or feel the textures that would give them a more complete understanding. In the story, one prisoner escapes. He stumbles out of the cave, blinded by the actual sun, and slowly starts to see things in the third dimension for the first time. He experiences the world like it truly is, not just shadows on the wall. This journey from Flatland to a fuller picture is exactly what Sousanis talks about - moving beyond the limited perspectives to see the richness and complexity of everything around us. So, the next time you feel like you are seeing only one side of the story, think of the cave prisoners. Do not get stuck in Flatland. Explore different viewpoints, question assumptions, seek answers and take a step outside your comfort zone. In doing so, you might just escape the cave and see the world in in own true light. This is the reality for the prisoners in Plato's Allegory of the Cave, who mistake these shadows for the true essence of the world. It is a reminder that our understanding of the world is shaped by our perspectives and experiences, and by embracing diverse viewpoints, we can enrich our understanding and appreciation of the world around us.
